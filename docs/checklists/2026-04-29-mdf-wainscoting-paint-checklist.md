# mdf wainscoting paint checklist

Use this for **raw MDF wainscoting** when the goal is a **smooth, cabinet-adjacent finish**.

## material and setup checklist
- [ ] confirm all visible pieces are cut to final size before finishing
- [ ] label the back of each piece so reinstall/layout is easy after painting
- [ ] verify the MDF is raw, not factory-primed
- [ ] choose a **shellac-based primer** for sealing raw MDF
- [ ] choose a **waterborne alkyd** or **alkyd-urethane enamel** for the topcoat
- [ ] choose sheen based on the nearby cabinets
  - [ ] satin if the cabinets are smoother but not very shiny
  - [ ] semi-gloss if you want a more trim/cabinet-like look
  - [ ] gloss only if the cabinets are clearly glossy and the prep is extremely clean
- [ ] confirm primer/topcoat compatibility from the product label or technical data sheet
- [ ] gather materials
  - [ ] 180–220 grit sandpaper
  - [ ] 320 grit sandpaper
  - [ ] vacuum, tack cloth, or clean lint-free rags
  - [ ] filler for nail holes and small defects
  - [ ] paintable caulk for install-stage seams
  - [ ] spray equipment and strainers
  - [ ] sawhorses, painter pyramids, or another way to keep pieces off the table
  - [ ] respirator, gloves, and ventilation

## prep checklist
- [ ] inspect every piece for dents, chips, swollen corners, or machining marks
- [ ] fill dents, chips, and visible defects
- [ ] let filler dry fully
- [ ] lightly sand the faces with 180–220 grit
- [ ] sand cut edges slightly more than the faces to knock down fuzziness
- [ ] ease any sharp corners very lightly so paint can hold better on edges
- [ ] remove all sanding dust thoroughly

## edge-sealing checklist
- [ ] inspect all cut edges and routed profiles for fuzziness or porosity
- [ ] spray or brush a sealing coat of shellac-based primer onto **all raw edges first**
- [ ] let the edge primer dry fully
- [ ] lightly sand the primed edges smooth
- [ ] check whether the edges still look thirsty or rough
- [ ] apply a second edge-sealing coat anywhere the MDF still looks porous
- [ ] sand again lightly if needed

## full-prime checklist
- [ ] strain the primer if needed for spraying
- [ ] spray a light, even coat of shellac-based primer on the show faces and profiles
- [ ] avoid flooding edges or inside corners
- [ ] let primer dry fully per the label
- [ ] inspect for raised fibers, rough spots, or missed areas
- [ ] sand the primed surfaces smooth with 220 grit
- [ ] move to 320 grit on the most visible faces if you want a finer finish
- [ ] remove all dust completely
- [ ] spot-prime any sand-throughs, patches, or exposed raw MDF
- [ ] apply a second full primer coat if the surface still looks uneven or porous
- [ ] sand the final primer coat smooth and clean off dust

## pre-topcoat inspection checklist
- [ ] look across the surface in raking light
- [ ] confirm there are no fuzzy edges, filler ridges, dust nibs, or heavy spray texture
- [ ] fix defects now instead of hoping the topcoat hides them
- [ ] test one sample or offcut with the topcoat before committing all parts if product combination is new

## topcoat checklist
- [ ] confirm temperature and humidity are within the paint manufacturer's recommended range
- [ ] strain the topcoat for spraying if needed
- [ ] spray the first coat of **waterborne alkyd** or **alkyd-urethane enamel**
- [ ] keep coats even and controlled; do not try to get full build in one pass
- [ ] let the first coat dry fully
- [ ] inspect for dust nibs, roughness, or dry spray
- [ ] sand lightly with 320 grit or finer if needed
- [ ] remove all dust carefully
- [ ] spray the second coat
- [ ] inspect sheen and smoothness after the second coat
- [ ] apply a third coat only if coverage, build, or sheen still needs improvement

## before-install checklist
- [ ] let the finished pieces dry enough to handle without imprinting
- [ ] protect the finished faces during transport and install
- [ ] plan for touch-up material to handle nail holes, seams, and minor install damage

## install and touch-up checklist
- [ ] install carefully to avoid chipping finished edges
- [ ] fill nail holes after installation
- [ ] caulk only the seams/gaps that should visually disappear
- [ ] keep caulk lines tight and neat because shinier paint highlights sloppy caulk
- [ ] spot-prime any exposed filler or damaged raw MDF
- [ ] apply final touch-up coats where needed
- [ ] feather touch-ups so sheen stays as even as possible

## cure checklist
- [ ] verify the paint is dry before normal handling
- [ ] verify the paint has had enough cure time before heavy cleaning or abrasion
- [ ] avoid taping to the finish too early
- [ ] keep the room reasonably clean while the finish hardens

## touch-up kit checklist
- [ ] keep a **4 inch high-density foam mini roller** on hand for smooth touch-ups
- [ ] keep a **4 inch or 6 inch mini roller with 1/4 inch nap microfiber cover** on hand in case the topcoat lays out better with microfiber than foam
- [ ] keep a **1.5 inch to 2 inch soft synthetic angled sash brush** on hand for profiles, corners, and tight areas

## touch-up method checklist
- [ ] let the sprayed finish cure enough that touch-up work will not drag or print into soft paint
- [ ] clean dust and debris from the damaged area
- [ ] if there is a raised chip or rough edge, sand it lightly until the defect is flat and controlled
- [ ] fill nail holes, dents, or small chips with a paintable filler suitable for trim work
- [ ] let filler dry fully
- [ ] sand the filled spot smooth with 220 grit, then refine with 320 grit if needed
- [ ] remove all dust from the repair area
- [ ] spot-prime any exposed raw MDF or filler, especially on edges or corners
- [ ] sand the primer lightly if it leaves texture
- [ ] use the **soft synthetic angled sash brush** for narrow profiles, inside corners, and small edge repairs
- [ ] use the **foam mini roller** first for flat areas if you want the least texture
- [ ] switch to the **1/4 inch nap microfiber mini roller** if the paint is not leveling well with foam or if the foam leaves bubbles
- [ ] use very small amounts of paint and build coverage gradually
- [ ] feather the repair outward so there is no hard paint ridge
- [ ] do not keep brushing or rolling after the paint starts to tack up
- [ ] if a touch-up is flashing or standing out too much, recoat the whole board, section, or panel instead of only the tiny patch
- [ ] let the repair dry and inspect it in angled light before deciding whether it is finished

## finish-choice notes
- [ ] use **shellac-based primer** because raw MDF and especially its edges need aggressive sealing
- [ ] use **waterborne alkyd** or **alkyd-urethane enamel** if the goal is a smoother, harder, more cabinet-like finish than standard wall paint
- [ ] expect sprayed wainscoting to look very good, but not necessarily identical to factory-finished cabinets
- [ ] if the cabinets are very glossy, consider stopping at **satin** or **semi-gloss** on the wainscoting unless your prep and spray setup are excellent

## stop-and-fix conditions
- [ ] stop if edges still look fuzzy after priming; they need more sealing/sanding
- [ ] stop if primer is not bonding or is reacting badly with the topcoat; re-check product compatibility
- [ ] stop if heavy orange peel, runs, or dry spray appear; correct technique/setup before finishing the rest
- [ ] stop if dust contamination is building into the finish; clean the area before the next coat
